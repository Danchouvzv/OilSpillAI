The Problem

Oil spills are catastrophic environmental events that severely impact marine ecosystems. They cause the death of countless sea animals and plant life, disrupt natural habitats, and contaminate water sources for years.

A significant challenge with oil spills is their long-lasting impact. Toxic substances persist in the environment, making it difficult for ecosystems to recover. Oil spills harm fisheries, disrupt local economies, and threaten human health through contaminated food and water sources.
